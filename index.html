The Suffering of Young and Emerging Adults

It's undeniable. Even if the media weren't replete with stories, we cannot avoid this reality. It is everywhere we look... in our schools, in our neighborhoods and in our own lives. Anxiety, Depression and Suicide are words that are too commonly associated with teens and young adults. What is happening? Are we creating weaker generations or is it something else?


On being 18 in 2018

Taking an honest assessment of the pressures that assail young adults is helpful. While life has its way of wearing us down, assuming that the high school graduate of today is facing the same pressures as he did 30 years ago is a bold assumption, and possibly a naive one. Let's do some exploring.

Attending College

As a society, we convey the message that a college degree is vital to a kid's future. without debating the truth of this message, let's look at the pressure it creates.

  • The cost of a degree has sky rocketed, around 300% of the inflation adjusted cost of a degree in 1968
  • Take dual credit classes in high school because they run about a third of the cost
  • Pursuing Scholarships is a daunting endeavor, requiring students to devise ways of differentiating themselves
    • GPA grade inflation so rampant that some schools refuse to report class rank
    SAT / ACT scores test prep industry poised to exceed $900 million soon
    special talent/skill athletic, artistic, or unique interest
    leadership officer or founding member of club or other organization
    awards list of times student has out performed others
    commitment to community service most HS graduation requirements include this. Students have to log in more to differentiate
    work history must show that they take fiscal responsibility seriously
  • No room for failure because every credit hour is so expensive
  • Post College Debt suffocating. The average student will leave college with $40,000 of debt, whether they leave with a degree or not

Univerisity professionals report that the students arriving on their campuses today are significantly less mature than even 10 years ago. Why wouldn't they be? The demands put on them will supercede some of the less obvious actions that lead to maturity: making mistakes, personal reflection, and habits of self care and support. So, the students, who are not stupid, know that in some very significant way, they are not ready for this transistion, a realization that simply intensifies the pressure to not fail.


Social Media

Being a digital native lends itself to a completely different experience from that of a digital immigrant. What does that statement mean? It means that an individual who comes of age using a smart phone and interacting with social media has relationships with those entities that significantly intense and consuming. For those who grew up before the digital age and have merely encorporated these devices and applications into an already established life workflow, the effect is less significant.

How does reconciling a virtual self with an actual self affect the well-being of a 14 year old?

What sort of neural pathways and relational norms are established during the digital assimilation of kids ages 12 to 18?

What will be the long-term consequences of an unparalleled development of self-absorption, to our Emerging adults and to our society?

Why are we so surprised by the decline of mental wellness?
