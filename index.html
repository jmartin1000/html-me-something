Attending College

Culturally, we convey the message that a college degree is vital to a kid's future. without debating the truth of this message, let's look at the pressure it creates.

  • The cost of a degree has sky rocketed, around 300% of the inflation adjusted cost of a degree in 1968
  • Take dual credit classes in high school because they run about a third of the cost
  • No room for failure because every credit hour is so expensive
  • Pursuing Scholarships is a daunting endeavor, requiring students to devise ways of differentiating themselves

Social Media

Being a digital native lends itself to a completely different experience from that of a digital immigrant. What does that statement mean? It means that an individual who comes of age using a smart phone and interacting with social media has relationships with those entities that significantly intense and consuming. For those who grew up before the digital age and have merely encorporated these devices and applications into an already established life workflow, the effect is less significant.

Our real self rarely conforms to the digital representation we create. Our avatar self always knows what to say and always looks appealing while saying it. How does reconciling a virtual self to an actual self affect the well-being of a 14 year old?
Branched Realities
What effect does the digital assimilation of kids from 12 to 18 have on the development of the brain?
The Brain
Self centeredness tends to diminish as we mature, perhaps never eradicated, but usually tempered and redirected. Maintaining a self promotion channel, on several platforms, hinders that type of maturity. What are the long term consequences to individuals and society of cultivating an intensely self absorbed population?
Self Absorption
Striking up a conversation with a stranger. Making new friends. Making and keeping plans. Talking to a crush. Asking awkward questions. Self advocating. All these are harder when you have only learned to socialize through a digital device.
Diminished Social Skills
How can we have any discussions about mental wellness without tackling these issues?
Mental Wellness

Dating and Relationships

Who hasn't heard the term Hookup Culture? The question really is who knows what it means? It is a set of rules and expectations that currently shape the relationships, both romantic and otherwise, of most young adults. Hookup is purposefully vague term that refers to a kiss and everything beyond. A full understanding of Hookup Culture requires a commitment of time and emotional vulnerability. Let's just consider some of the implications of a social script that dictates casual sexual encounters and prohibits the development of intimacy.

Hookup culture is not the same as having freedom to explore sexuality. This is a social construction based on using other people for personal satisfaction and social status. These are selfish pursuits without any real consideration for the dignity of others. Additionally, since many participants educate themselves by watching pornography, the accepted norm for sexual interaction is skewed in a violent way. When alcohol becomes part of this equation, it can have devastating consequences.
Sexual Violence
One of the tenets of Hookup Culture is to be totally self sufficient and reliant. This extends to all relationsihps. Students report wide-spread feelings of loneliness and isolation as a consequence of Hookup Culture. Even individuals who opt out are left with a sense of being on their own island that no one will ever visit.
Isolation
Those who are able to find a relationship of some level of intimacy, often remain in relationships with an imbalance of power. Their rationale is that a bad relationship is better than no relationship. Will we soon see a rise in domestic abuse cycles?
Relationship Abuse
Young Adults do not date. For many reasons. This does not mean they do not want to date. On the contrary, research indicates that most young adults would like to date. They feel this is no longer an option because the culture prohibits it and because they do not believe they will find another person who feels the same way.
Dating is a Relic
